Know all about the Google Glass

by Gustavo Guanabara

Updated April/23th/2013

What is?

O Google Glass is an accessory in the form of glasses that allows users to interact with diverse content in augmented reality. Also calledProject Glass, the electronic is capable of taking pictures using voice commands, send instant messages and conduct video­conferences. Its launch is scheduled for 2014, and its price should be US $ 1,500. Currently theGoogle Glass is in the testing phase and already has a fully recorded video with the device. In addition, the search company has registered new anti-theft and screen unlock patents for the accessory.

Release date of:

There is no specific and official date for the device to be released, yet. It may be available in demonstrations starting in 2013, but its launch for stores is for at least 2014.

How Works?:

According to sources close to Google, the glasses will have a small LCD or AMOLED screen at the top and in front of the user's eyes. With the use of a camera and GPS, you can position yourself, as well as select options with the movement of the head.

What can you do with the Google Glasses?

The publicity video from Google shows that you can become a kind of “super-human ”, since the device can indicate how many meters you are from your destination, whether the metro is open or closed, show the weather, schedule and even allow you to make appointments with just voice commands.

Newness in the Glass:

Google finally revealed the complete specifications for Google Glass,   and with it a surprise still unprecedented in the market:   the search giant will use an audio system based on conduction transduction.   Through the glasses' stems, the sound will be transmitted to the user's ear by   by means of microvibrations in certain bones of your head,   without using any kind of speaker.

In addition to the audio surprise, the screen mounted in front of the user's eye also drew attention. It will be 640 x 360 pixels of resolution that, in proportion, would be equivalent to a 25 inch monitor high definition TV placed 2.5 meters away from the viewer..
